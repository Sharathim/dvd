Understanding RERA and Its Importance for Home Buyers

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act (RERA), enacted in 2016, is a game changer for the real estate sector in India. Designed to protect home buyers and bring transparency to the industry, RERA has empowered buyers by providing them with a secure and informed environment in their dream homes.

Under RERA, developers are required to register projects with the state authority before sale, disclose project status and approvals, maintain separate escrow accounts, and adhere strictly to promised timelines. This framework reduces risks, improves accountability, and gives buyers a clear legal path in case of delays or non-compliance.

At DV Constructions, RERA compliance is a guarantee of trust. We ensure all projects adhere to RERA guidelines, delivering homes with legal integrity, financial transparency, and reliable information at every stage.

The Evolution of Real Estate in India: From 2000 to 2025

2000s

The Early 2000s: A Nascent Stage

At the turn of the millennium, real estate in India was still in its formative years. The sector was primarily dominated by local developers with limited standardization.

2005-2010

2005-2010: IT Boom and Urban Expansion

The mid-2000s marked rapid transformation led by IT and infrastructure growth. Urban centers saw integrated townships and luxury housing projects gain momentum.

2010-2015

2010-2015: Rising Consumer Expectations

Post 2010, affordability and quality became equally important. Developers introduced better amenities, modern architecture, and customer-focused offerings.

2016

2016: The RERA Revolution

RERA transformed the industry by mandating project registration, transparency, and strict developer accountability, bringing new confidence to buyers.

2017-2020

2017-2020: Technology and Sustainability

Virtual tours, digital documentation, smart homes, and green design principles became mainstream, redefining what buyers expect from modern homes.

2020-2025

2020-2025: Resilience and Future Growth

Despite pandemic-era disruption, demand rebounded with stronger trust, digital workflows, and innovation. Indian real estate is now poised for sustainable growth.
